Once upon a time, in the ancient Yayoi Period ruins, archaeologists stumbled upon some peculiar carbonized rice clumps that looked suspiciously like modern-day onigiri! Yes, folks, it seems like the love for these ricey delights dates back eons! But hold your chopsticks, because back then, they weren't quite the triangular wonders we know today. No, sir! They were more like rice-packed bundles, wrapped in bamboo leaves, looking like mini corn cobs - boiled or steamed to perfection.

Fast forward to the Heian Period, and we encounter a curious concoction called "Ton-jiki." Picture this - brown rice squished into the shape of an egg and handed out to the peeps at fancy imperial and noble events. "Ton" meant "to collect and bundle" or "instant" - so, it was the OG fast food, way before drive-thrus were a thing!

As time marched on, onigiri became a lifesaver for folks during the Sengoku Period. It was the ultimate snack for warriors on the go and the go-to "bento" for hardworking farmers. Sticky, glutinous rice was the star back then, but eventually, regular rice joined the party, making onigiri more accessible to everyone.

Now, the true magic happened during the Edo Period! The genius move was wrapping those rice goodies in "nori" (seaweed). Picture this - perfectly processed, rectangular seaweed laver taking the stage. It was all the rage during the Genroku Era, making onigiri look oh-so-cool. Suddenly, onigiri started showing up everywhere - at firework displays, cherry blossom viewing parties, and even on travelers' adventures!

Time whizzed by, and in the late 1800s, train passengers got their hands on the first-ever bento lunches at Utsunomiya Station. Two onigiri buddies, accompanied by pickled plums and daikon pickles, were the star attractions. The Meiji Period saw onigiri taking over school lunch menus, next to some delicious grilled fish and more pickles. Yum!

But wait, it gets even better! Enter the 1970s, and convenience stores popped up across Japan. They knew the key to every busy person's heart - onigiri! Wrapped in a nifty nori sheet, these little rice bundles became the go-to grab-and-go option. And oh boy, they struck gold when they invented an easy way to wrap onigiri with nori - everybody went wild for it!

The onigiri love affair continues to this day, deeply embedded in the hearts and tummies of the Japanese folks. You'll find them everywhere - a staple food, a travel buddy, and even a comforting meal after disasters. And just when you thought onigiri couldn't get any cooler, along came "onigirazu"! From ancient rice clumps to portable, seaweed-wrapped delights - onigiri has truly stood the test of time, and we're all here for it!
